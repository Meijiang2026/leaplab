Led by Dr. Mei Jiang and Dr. Shifang Tang, LEAP Lab is a research lab at the intersection of educational psychology and artificial intelligence — studying how people learn with, from, and alongside AI systems, and how we can design those systems to truly serve human flourishing.

FULLY VIRTUAL LAB

ABOUT THE LAB

Why LEAP?

AI is reshaping classrooms, tutoring systems, and the very way students construct knowledge. Yet the psychological science of how people actually learn with AI is still in its infancy. LEAP Lab exists to close that gap.

We draw on cognitive science, motivation theory, and learning analytics to ask rigorous questions: Does AI feedback build self-regulation or erode it? How does human-AI collaboration change metacognition? What makes AI tutors equitable — or not?

LEAP Lab is co-directed by Dr. Mei Jiang and Dr. Shifang Tang, both faculty in Educational Psychology. As a fully virtual lab, we believe geography should never be a barrier to great science. Our team collaborates asynchronously and synchronously across time zones, bringing together diverse perspectives and lived experiences into every research question we pursue.

JOIN THE LAB

We're Recruiting

We welcome students who are curious, rigorous, and excited about working at an unusual disciplinary frontier. You don't need to arrive with all the answers — just a genuine drive to ask better questions. No prior experience with research is necessary.

Two Ways to Join the Lab

OPTION 1 — INDEPENDENT RESEARCHER

  • Conduct your own study as a thesis, dissertation, or independent project Develop a research question, design your study, and see it through to completion Receive faculty mentorship at every stage of the research process Open to both undergraduate and graduate students

OPTION 2 — RESEARCH ASSISTANT

  • Choose to volunteer in the lab or enroll in PSY 411, 412, or 413 (Research Apprenticeship) with instructor permission for course credit Assist with all phases of empirical research — literature review, study design, data collection, coding, and analysis Co-author reports and conference presentations

All lab members receive systematic research training through hands-on participation in empirical studies — from hypothesis to findings.
